The Fair Launch Handbook

When we talk about a fair launch, we refer to a token distribution mechanic that's centered around equity, transparency, and inclusivity. This is a distinct departure from methods such as Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs) and other distribution strategies that might involve pre-sales, private sales, or allocations to early investors and team members, all of which have the potential to skew distribution and centralize tokens.

The concept of a fair launch is essentially a response to these traditional methods of token distribution that can lead to centralization. By ensuring equal opportunities and a level playing field for all participants, a fair launch aims to create a more community-centric ecosystem. The primary goal is to give all participants equal opportunities, with no unfair benefits or preferential access. The distribution process is designed to be as inclusive as possible, allowing anyone who wants to participate and contribute to the community to do so.

No Presales, No Private Sales, No ICOs

These elements are not part of a fair launch strategy. The aim is to make sure everyone gets the same chance to acquire tokens.

Simultaneous Start

The launch of the token happens at a particular time that's set and announced publicly beforehand. This allows everyone to have an equal chance to participate right from the get-go.

Transparency

All the rules and mechanisms of token distribution are made crystal clear and readily available to the entire community. This transparency extends to various elements, such as token allocation, supply, distribution methods, and any vesting or lock-up periods that might be in place.

No Pre-allocation

With a fair launch, there's a concerted effort to avoid pre-allocating a substantial portion of tokens to a select group of individuals or entities before the launch. This principle helps ensure that early participants don't end up with an unfair advantage.

Token Generation

Tokens are usually generated through processes such as a decentralized exchange (DEX) listing or the creation of a liquidity pool. This means that participants can acquire tokens by either trading or providing liquidity - a strategy often referred to as liquidity mining.

Community Participation

Fair launches often prioritize active community involvement and engagement. Various measures to encourage this might be put in place, such as community governance, voting mechanisms, or incentives for active participation.
